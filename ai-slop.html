AI Slop

How to recognize low-quality AI output and keep your work sharp.

AI slop is low-effort, generic, repetitive content that looks polished at first glance but says very little. It shows up in blog posts, code, product copy, design, and even entire apps — and the faster you can spot it, the less of it you'll ship.

01 How to spot AI slop

Slop has tells. Once you've seen a few, they jump out. The four classic signs:

It sounds confident, but empty

Slop leans on broad statements like "in today's fast-paced world" or "unlock your full potential" without adding anything concrete. The sentence is grammatically perfect and means nothing.

It repeats itself

If the same idea shows up in three different phrasings, the content is padding — not communicating. The model ran out of things to say and started echoing itself.

It avoids specifics

Real writing names tradeoffs, examples, numbers, and constraints. Slop stays safely generic because specifics are risky, and the model defaults to safe.

It has no opinion

Good content makes a point. Slop tries to please everyone and ends up saying nothing memorable.

🚩 Rule of thumb

If you wouldn't publish it after a quick human edit, it's probably slop. The polish is the trap — it feels finished precisely because it's been smoothed into nothing.

02 Why AI slop happens

Slop isn't a bug in the model. It's what you get when the process rewards volume over judgment:

  • People ask the AI for a finished result instead of a rough draft.
  • Teams optimize for speed and output instead of usefulness.
  • Nobody edits for voice, accuracy, or the actual audience.
  • Prompts are vague, so the output is vague too.
  • There's no human taste layer deciding what should be cut.

03 How to avoid making it

The fix isn't using AI less — it's using it differently. Treat the model as a starting point, not a finish line:

  • Start with a clear audience and a single goal.
  • Ask for an outline, not a final answer, when the topic matters.
  • Provide examples, constraints, and real source material.
  • Cut filler aggressively. If a sentence can go, it should.
  • Replace generic language with specifics — names, numbers, real details.
  • Add your own point of view. That's the part the model can't fake.
  • Read it out loud. Robotic phrasing is obvious when spoken.
  • Prefer fewer, better outputs over many mediocre ones.
✅ The mindset

AI should reduce blank-page friction, not replace judgment. Taste is a feature — it's the only thing standing between a draft and actual slop.

04 Slop vs. something better

The difference is concrete. Here's the same idea written two ways:

"Our innovative solution leverages cutting-edge AI to transform your workflow and drive unprecedented results."
Better

"This tool summarizes customer calls into action items, so your team can follow up faster and stop losing notes in Slack."

The second one names a real job, a real tool, and a real pain. The first one could describe anything, which means it describes nothing.

Got the content sharp? Make sure the code and the launch are too.
