🧠 Quality control for the AI era

AI slop is what happens when “good enough” becomes the whole strategy.

AI slop is low-effort, generic, repetitive content that looks polished at first glance but says very little. It can show up in blog posts, code, product copy, design, and even entire apps.

Quick definition

AI slop Content or output that is fast to generate, easy to skim, and hard to trust.
Common signs Vague claims, repeated phrasing, fake specificity, and no real point of view.
Best defense Use AI as a draft partner, then edit for clarity, evidence, and taste.

How to spot AI slop

It sounds confident, but empty

Slop often uses broad statements like “in today’s fast-paced world” or “unlock your full potential” without adding anything concrete.

It repeats itself

If the same idea appears in three different ways, the content may be padding instead of communicating.

It avoids specifics

Real writing names tradeoffs, examples, numbers, and constraints. Slop stays safely generic.

It has no opinion

Good content makes a point. Slop tries to please everyone and ends up saying nothing memorable.

Why AI slop happens

  • People ask AI for a finished result instead of a rough draft.
  • Teams optimize for speed and volume instead of usefulness.
  • No one edits for voice, accuracy, or audience.
  • Prompts are vague, so the output is vague too.
  • There’s no human taste layer to decide what should be cut.
Rule of thumb: if you wouldn’t publish it after a quick human edit, it’s probably slop.

How to avoid making it

  • Start with a clear audience and goal.
  • Ask for an outline, not a final answer, when the topic matters.
  • Provide examples, constraints, and source material.
  • Cut filler aggressively.
  • Check facts and replace generic language with real details.
  • Add your own point of view.
  • Use AI to brainstorm, summarize, and draft, then rewrite.
  • Read it out loud to catch robotic phrasing.
  • Prefer fewer, better outputs over many mediocre ones.
  • Remember: taste is a feature.
Best practice: AI should reduce blank-page friction, not replace judgment.

Examples of the difference

Slop

“Our innovative solution leverages cutting-edge AI to transform your workflow and drive unprecedented results.”

Better

“This tool summarizes customer calls into action items, so your team spends less time taking notes and more time following up.”

Slop

“Here are 10 game-changing tips to supercharge your productivity.”

Better

“Here are three changes that actually help: batch email twice a day, write next steps immediately, and stop switching tabs every five minutes.”

AI is a tool. Taste, editing, and judgment are still the product.
